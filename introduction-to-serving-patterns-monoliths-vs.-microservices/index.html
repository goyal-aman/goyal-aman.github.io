Introduction to Serving Patterns: Monoliths vs. Microservices

10 Jan, 2026

The Big Picture Shift

  • We are moving from single-node patterns to multi-node distributed systems
  • Systems are now loosely coupled, communicate via network APIs, and use parallel or asynchronous coordination

Monolith vs. Microservices

  • Monolith: Everything is in one application/container, resulting in tight coupling and hard selective scaling
  • Microservices: The app is split into small, independent services communicating via APIs

Why Microservices Are Useful

  • Better team ownership: Small “two-pizza” teams can own specific services
  • Decoupling via APIs: Services interact through stable contracts, allowing independent development and deployment
  • Abstraction and Encapsulation: Implementation details are hidden, reducing complexity and improving productivity
  • Independent scaling: You can scale stateless services horizontally or shard stateful ones as needed

Downsides of Microservices

  • Harder debugging: Failures span multiple services and machines
  • Higher complexity: Requires many communication patterns and coordination mechanisms
  • Over-fragmentation: Too many small services can be detrimental; follow the rule of thumb that you shouldn’t have more services than engineers
  • Overhead: Network calls and multiple services increase CPU, memory, and performance costs

One-line Summary

Microservices break applications into independently deployable services that communicate over APIs, improving scalability and team autonomy—but at the cost of higher system complexity and debugging difficulty.
